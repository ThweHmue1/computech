Basic Excel Tasks

Key words
Term Definition
cell A box ona spreadsheet in which you enter a single piece of data. Cells are identified by the column and row, e.g. cell E12 = column E and row 12.
column A range of vertical cells identified by a letter.
formula A calculation using numbers, mathematical operations and cells in a spreadsheet.
function A piece of code within a spreadsheet program that performs a calculation.
range A number of cells across a column, row or both.
row A range of horizontal cells identified by a number.
worksheet A named set of rows and columns. There can be more than one worksheet in a workbook.

Starting the new spreadsheet

When you start a new spreadsheet, the title is Book 1 - Excel. Remember to click on the title and give your spreadsheet a suitable filename.

At the bottom of the spreadsheet, you will see a tab called Sheet 1. This is the name of the current worksheet in your spreadsheet. You can rename this by right clicking on Sheet 1 and choosing Rename.

A spreadsheet can have more than one worksheet. Click on the + at the bottom of the sheet to add a new worksheet to your spreadsheet.

Adding The Data

Each cell in a spreadsheet can hold data in the form of numbers, letters, symbols or formulas.

When you click on a cell, a green outline will appear around it. This is called the active cell. The name of the cell will be shown near the top left corner of the sheet. The cell is named with the letter of the column followed by the number of the row it is in.

Click once on an empty cell to add data. You will notice that different sorts of data are set to be aligned differently in the cells. Letters are aligned to the left of the cell. Numbers are aligned to the right of the cell.

Once you have added data in a cell, you can press the Enter key to move down to the next cell in the row. If you want to move across to the cell in the next column, you can press the Tab key instead. There are two main ways to move around in a Sheet to get to the cell you want to enter data into.

1. Use the mouse to click in any celL.

2. Use the arrow keys on the keyboard to move left, right, up or down.

To edit data already entered in a cell, double-click on the cell.

Resizing Rows and Columns

To make rows or columns larger, hover the cursor between the row numbers or column letters. As you move between two different letters or numbers, the cursor will change to show a line with a double headed arrow. Click and drag to make the row or column larger or smaller.

Genral Formatting

Use the format tools on the shortcut toolbar to change the appearance of cells, rows or columns. You can format and align text, fill cells with colour, add gridlines and wrap text. Wrapping text means breaking a long line of text into more than one line so that it fits inside the cell without overflowing.

Formatting Numbers

Spreadsheets can contain different types of information. These different types of information may include: dates, numbers with decimal places, currency or times. You can format how you want numbers to appear in the cells. This will save you time when you are typing in your data.

Click and drag to select the cells the numbers are going to appear in

Go to the Number formatting section of the shortcut toolbar and click on the arrow at the end of the General box. A list of different number formats will appear and you can choose the one you want to use.

Some of the most common formats are included on the shortcut toolbar to make this even easier.

Sorting Data

Spreadsheets can be used to sort data quickly and easily. This can make the data easier to read and understand.

Sorting Data into Alphabetical or Numerical Order

Top Tip: Make sure that each column has a title cell at the top.

To sort a column of data into order, click in the title cell at the top of the column you want to sort. Select Data from the menu toolbar.

Using Function

Spreadsheets become powerful when functions are used to do calculations quickly. Functions are formulas that are built into spreadsheets ready for you to use.

Two of the most common functions are:

SUM - adding up a range of cells.

AVERAGE- finding the average of a range of cells.

Finding the SUM of a Set of Numbers

Click and drag to select the cells you want to add together. On the shortcut toolbar, click on the arrow at the end of the AutoSum icon. Choose sUM from the list of commonly used functions.

The total sum of all the numbers selected will now appear in the cell at the bottom of the column.

When you click in the cell that has a function added to it, the function will appear in the Formula Bar at the top of the Sheet.

Once the SUM function has been added, you can change any of the numbers in the cells above it and the cell containing the formula will automatically update to show the new total.

Finding the AVERAGE of a Set of Number

Click and drag to select the cells you want to find the average of. On the shortcut toolbar, click on the arrow at the end of the AutoSum icon. Choose AVERAGE from the list of commonly used functions. The average of the numbers selected will be calculated and displayed in the cell at the bottom of the column.

When you click on the cell which the function was added to, you will see the function displayed in the Formula Bar at the top of the Sheet.

Once the AVERAGE function has been added, you can change any of the numbers in the cells above it and the cell containing the formula will automatically update.

Creating a Chart

Click and drag across the cells to choose the range of data you want to show in a chart.

Go to Insert on the menu toolbar, and choose the chart type. Click on the arrow at the end of each chart type to see the different styles available. Click on the one you want to use. You may also notice a small chart icon appearing at the bottom right corner of your cells.

You may also notice a small chart icon appearing at the bottom right corner of your cells. This is the Quick Analysis Tool. Click on this icon and select Charts as another way to create a chart.

Editing a Chart

To edit the chart, click to select it. Handles will appear around the chart. You can move the chart by clicking and dragging, or use the handles to resize the chart. When the chart is selected, three new icons appear. The top icon can be used to edit the title of the chart and label the axes.

Right-click on a bar or segment to change the colour.
